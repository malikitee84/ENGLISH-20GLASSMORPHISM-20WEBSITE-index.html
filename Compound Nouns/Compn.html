Compound Nouns

Definition: Compound nouns are nouns that are formed by combining two or more separate words to create a new word with a distinct meaning. These words are often used together to refer to a single entity, concept, or object. Compound nouns can be written as separate words, hyphenated words, or single words, depending on the specific language and grammatical rules.


Example: Brother-in-law

Compound Noun can be a combination of:

Combination Example
Noun + Noun Classroom
Adjective + Noun Chief Minister
Swimming + Noun Chief Minister

Note: Always check the Root word to form the plural.
